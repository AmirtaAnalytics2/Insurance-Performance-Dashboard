## Business Problem Statement

Insurance organizations handle large volumes of customer policies, premium collections, claims processing, renewals, and risk management activities. Without a centralized reporting system, it becomes difficult for business leaders to track operational performance, monitor claim exposure, identify inactive customers, and measure revenue contribution across policy categories.

The objective of this dashboard is to provide management with a single interactive view of the insurance business, enabling faster decision-making through real-time visibility into premiums, claims, customer segments, and policy status.

This solution helps answer critical business questions such as:

* Which insurance products generate maximum premium revenue?
* What is the current claim liability against collected premium?
* How many policies are active vs inactive?
* Which customer age group contributes higher claim costs?
* What is the status of claims (Pending / Settled / Rejected)?
* Which policy categories require strategic attention?

By using this dashboard, leadership teams can improve profitability, optimize claim operations, increase customer retention, and strengthen portfolio performance.

## Project Objective

Designed and developed an end-to-end Power BI dashboard to transform raw insurance data into meaningful business insights for executives, operations teams, and analysts.

## Steps Followed

### Data Preparation

* Imported insurance datasets from structured source files.
* Used Power Query Editor for data cleansing and transformation.
* Removed duplicates, handled missing values, and standardized column formats.
* Corrected data types for numeric, currency, text, and date fields.
* Validated data quality using column profiling and distribution checks.

### Data Modeling

* Built relationships between customer, policy, and claims datasets.
* Created an optimized reporting model.

### DAX Measures Created

* Total Premium Amount
* Total Coverage Amount
* Total Claim Amount
* Active Policies Count
* Inactive Policies Count
* Claim Status Counts
* Premium by Policy Type
* Claim Amount by Age Group

### Dashboard Development

* Added slicers for Policy Number, Claim Number, and Customer ID.
* Created KPI cards for Premium, Coverage, and Claim Amount.
* Developed visuals for premium trends, claims status, age-group analysis, and policy matrix.
* Applied professional formatting with dark theme and interactive navigation.

## Executive Summary

The dashboard reveals a strong premium base with multiple performing policy categories. Claims rejection and pending volumes indicate opportunities to improve claims operations and customer experience.

Travel and Health policies are leading premium contributors, while inactive customers present retention opportunities.

## Key Business Insights

### Revenue Insights

* Travel insurance contributes the highest premium revenue.
* Health and Auto are additional strong-performing categories.
* Home and Life products show growth opportunities.

### Claims Insights

* Rejected claims are high and need root-cause analysis.
* Pending claims may impact customer satisfaction.
* Settled claims help monitor payout efficiency.

### Customer Insights

* Adult customers contribute the highest claim amount.
* Young Adult segment indicates lower-risk opportunity.

### Portfolio Insights

* Active policies exceed inactive policies.
* Inactive customers can be targeted through renewal campaigns.

## Strategic Recommendations

1. Focus on high-performing products like Travel and Health.
2. Reduce pending claims through process automation.
3. Analyze rejection reasons and improve documentation flow.
4. Launch retention campaigns for inactive policyholders.
5. Build targeted offers for low-risk customer segments.
6. Monitor claim-to-premium ratio for profitability.
